The collection.

The portfolio surface is where a firm surveys what it holds. Today it is a dashboard of KPIs and progress bars — metrics stacked without a point of view. The collection has no custodian, no seasons, no character. It is possible to read the whole screen and still not know which companies matter.

§ 01 Findings

Four things the collection isn't doing.

01 VoiceMedium

"KPI dashboard" is the wrong register.

The page opens with a row of four identical metric cards — Total Holdings, Active, Risk Events, ESG Score — all rendered in the same weight, same size, same colour. Nothing is asked of the reader. A portfolio surface is a curatorial view; it should lead with a sentence, not a leaderboard.

Rewrite
Lede with one sentence: "Fourteen holdings across three regions — two want attention this week." Then the numbers, displayed as editorial pull-stats, not cards.
02 GroupingHigh

All holdings look equal.

The table sorts alphabetically by default. A ten-year anchor holding sits next to a three-month-old watchlist stub, and nothing marks the difference. Stewardship requires hierarchy — core, watchlist, exiting — and a visible sense of how long each has been held.

Add
Three editorial groups with mono eyebrows: § held long, § under watch, § on the way out. Tenure appears beside each name in mono.
03 Progress barsMedium

The bars don't carry their weight.

Each holding has an ESG progress bar. But ESG is not a race toward 100 — it's a posture, and the bar format implies completion. Worse, the bars are all the same indigo, so a 92% ESG next to a 34% ESG reads as "two bars" rather than "a strength and a concern."

Replace
Drop the bars. Replace with a single editorial note per holding: "Carbon reporting clean — governance is where the work is." Ground truth in a sentence beats a gauge.
04 EmptyLow

A quiet portfolio should feel proud, not broken.

When no holdings match a filter, the view collapses to "No results. Try a different filter." A portfolio with no flagged items this week is a good portfolio — the surface should say so.

Rewrite
"Nothing on watch this week. The collection is quiet." — with a mono line of what was last reviewed.
