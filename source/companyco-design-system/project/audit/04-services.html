The arrangements.

Services are the ongoing arrangements a firm has with its providers — audit, tax, custody, AML, legal. Today they are rendered as a grid of activity cards with status chips. The list does not distinguish between a service that has been in place for fifteen years and one that was switched on last Tuesday. It does not tell you who, or since when.

§ 01 Findings

Three things the arrangements aren't doing.

01 StructureMedium

A card grid is the wrong container.

Each service sits in its own card with a status chip and a button. The grid makes it hard to scan — a reader has to visit every card to answer "who's my auditor?". The answer should be legible in a single glance.

Replace
A four-column ledger: arrangement · provider · state · since. One row per service, read top-to-bottom like a list of appointments.
02 ChipsMedium

Green / yellow / grey is too loud.

Status chips render as Active green, Paused yellow, Unset grey. For a screen that is mostly "active," the green chips repeat across every card and add visual noise without meaning. The chip is the default case; it should not need decoration.

Drop
Render state as an italic word in the ledger: active for normal, paused for held, — not engaged for unset. Colour only the exception.
03 Missing tenureHigh

There is no "since when."

The card shows the provider and the state. It does not show how long the arrangement has been in place — which is exactly the information a steward needs first. Tenure shapes trust. A ten-year auditor is not a ten-month auditor.

Add
A fourth column: since. Formatted in mono: since 2014 · 11 yrs.
