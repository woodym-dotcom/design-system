§ 01 Findings

Three things the ticket tracker gets wrong.

01 RegisterHigh

"INC-0142" is a helpdesk ID.

Each case is surfaced as a monospace ID with a short title — INC-0142 — Suspected duplicate officer. It gives no sense of the stakes or the arc of the investigation. Cases are not tickets; they are narratives with unfinished endings.

Rewrite
Keep the case number, but small. Lead with a sentence title: "Two names, one signature — Meridian and its mirror."
02 TabsMedium

Open / Triage / Closed is a workflow, not a view.

The tabs mirror a back-office workflow. A reviewer wants to know what's warming up, what's in the room, and what's behind us. Rename for that posture.

Rename
§ warming, § in the room, § closed. Same queues, different temperature.
03 ClosureMedium

"Resolved" is the blandest possible ending.

Closed cases are simply tagged Resolved. But the how matters enormously — was it merged, cleared, referred out, filed away? A case's ending shapes how the next one is opened.

Add
One-word outcome in italic serif: merged, cleared, referred, filed away.
