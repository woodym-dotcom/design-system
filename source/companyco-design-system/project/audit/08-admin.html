The household.

Admin is where a firm manages its own people — who can see what, who joined when, who hasn't signed in. The current surface is a settings panel with side-nav and tables. It gets the job done but it treats staff as rows. The tool's own tone should be warmest here.

§ 01 Findings

Two things the admin panel can soften.

01 FrameMedium

"Admin" is a back-office word.

The page title is literally "Admin", with subsections Users, Roles, Audit log. The language is borrowed from WordPress 2008. The firm isn't administering; it's keeping house. A name like the household sets the right tone without losing clarity.

Rewrite
Eyebrow § the household, with subsections the desk (users), what people can do (roles), what has been done (audit log).
02 Empty statesLow

"No users invited yet" is a dead end.

The Users tab shows "No users invited yet." The sentence closes the door. The empty state should open one — what's next, who typically gets invited first, how long it takes a new user to settle.

Rewrite
"Just you on the desk so far. Most firms start by inviting an auditor and a counsel — the rest tend to follow."
