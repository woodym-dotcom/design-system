The threshold.

The sign-in screen is the single surface that sets the tone before the product begins. Today it's a white box on a grey background. The brand deserves a threshold — a page that tells a new visitor, without a word of marketing, what kind of tool they're about to use.

§ 01 Findings

Two things the threshold is missing.

01 CompositionHigh

The page is one card on a field of grey.

A centred white box holds the form. The rest of the viewport is wasted. The brand — serif voice, mono accents, ink ground — never appears until after the first click. That's a missed introduction.

Compose
Split the page: ink-black canvas on the left with an editorial line in serif; a quiet sign-in column on the right, bordered in faint amber.
02 CopyMedium

"Welcome back" is every SaaS login.

The heading reads "Welcome back". Neutral, forgettable, indistinguishable from ten thousand others. A single sentence in the brand's voice — one line of the ETHOS — is a free way to set the register.

Rewrite
Left: "The stewardship of things you did not build." Right heading: "Come in."
