The mantel.

The app shell — the strip across the top — is the room's mantelpiece. What sits there sets the tone for everything below. Today it's a standard-issue SaaS bar: logo, workspace picker, search, bell, avatar. Each piece works. Together they say nothing.

§ 01 Findings

Three things the mantel is doing by default.

01 IdentityHigh

The logo is a wordmark in Inter.

The brand mark at top-left is set in the same sans as the UI. It disappears. Every other surface in the product uses the serif voice; the shell doesn't. That's the first thing to fix — the mantel should be where the brand is loudest.

Set the mark
Brand wordmark in Cormorant Garamond, "Co" in italic amber. Add an est. MMXX micro-line beneath in mono.
02 Workspace pickerMedium

"Acme Capital ⌄" is a dropdown.

The active company is presented as a form control. It's the most important context in the entire product — it decides what every other screen is about — and it's dressed like a select menu.

Feature it
Eyebrow YOU ARE WITH in mono. Company name in serif, one size larger than anything else in the shell.
03 Right sideLow

Search, bell, avatar — silver buttons.

The three icons on the right are rendered as bordered squares. Too loud. They should recede into the shell and only become visible when sought.

Strip
Vertical dividers between sections, no icon borders. Labels in mono micro-caps beside the glyphs: SEARCH, ON YOUR DESK, JAMES.
