A teardown · 12 of 14

The map.

The left rail is an icon-only strip. To learn the product, a new user has to hover every icon to read its tooltip. The rail is so narrow that the names of the hubs never appear. For a product this content-rich, hiding the map behind icons is an act of modesty the brand cannot afford.

§ 01 Findings

Two things the map refuses to do.

01 LabelsHigh

Icons alone aren't enough.

The rail has eight destinations. They're rendered as identical rounded squares with faintly different 20×20 glyphs. New users can't tell a portfolio from an incident without hovering each. Power users memorise by position. Neither is acceptable in a tool where the names of the hubs are part of the brand.

Widen and label
Rail to 200px. Hub names in Cormorant, lowercase. Icon shrinks to a 6px amber dot at the start of each row. Active row gets a filled dot and a faint amber panel.
02 NotificationsMedium

Red dots on icons.

Pending items are flagged with small red badges on the rail icons. The red jars against the calm of everything else, and count is stripped away. "Something over there" isn't enough information.

Replace
Right-aligned count in mono amber — 3, 2. No shapes, no colour wash.
