The drawer.

When a row in a table is clicked, a panel slides in from the right with the full record. It's the product's most-used secondary surface — every hub opens it. Today it renders as a white pane with a close button, a title, a status chip, and a stack of label-value rows. It's fine; it could be a drawer.

§ 01 Findings

Three things the panel should borrow from the brand.

01 HandleMedium

The panel has no handle.

It slides in, and there's nothing at its top edge to tell you where you are. The close button is alone at the right. A thin handle rail — a 28px strip with a section label and the close — frames the drawer and gives the interaction a sense of weight.

Add
A handle rail across the top: § COMPANY · LU · B-187432 on the left, a mono close × on the right.
02 TitleMedium

The title is sans-serif bold.

The entity name is rendered in Inter 16px semibold. This is the one place in the product where the user lingers on a single name — it deserves the Cormorant treatment.

Set
Title in Cormorant Garamond 24px, italic tail if the entity has a descriptor (e.g. Treasury BV).
03 RowsLow

The rows are a key-value table.

Label left, value right. Labels in grey, values in black. Competent. But the values are just text — names, dates, addresses. They could wear the serif and feel, as one of the ETHOS lines put it, held.

Style
Labels in mono micro-caps (amber). Values in italic serif for names, plain mono for identifiers. Grid 110 / 1fr.
