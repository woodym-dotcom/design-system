§ 01 Findings

Two things the placeholder need not do.

01 CopyLow

"Coming soon" is a concession, not a plan.

The paragraph reads "This area is coming soon. Check back later for updates." It's the two sentences every product ships and forgets. A placeholder should name the thing and say what's being planned — even briefly.

Rewrite
A one-sentence lede in serif: "A map of everything you've filed, in chronological order." Then three mono eyebrows with the planned pieces — sketched, not promised.
02 IconLow

The grey circle is not a brand.

The placeholder centres a grey circle with an icon inside. It dates instantly. Replace with the page's eyebrow treatment — mono tracked, small, signed at the bottom.

Remove
No icon. Eyebrow in mono at top. A small — in preparation signature at the bottom, in the editor's voice.
