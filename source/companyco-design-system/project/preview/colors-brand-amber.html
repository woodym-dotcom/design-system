Ink, ledger, paper.

A governance-grade identity for CompanyCo: Oxford ink instead of black, ledger green instead of amber, warm paper instead of luxury cream. The tone it signals — considered, institutional, audit-ready — matches the buyer (board directors, risk officers, regulators) without dressing the product in hospitality gloss.

01 · WHY INK

Authority, not luxury.

Oxford navy reads as institutional — the color of charter documents and central-bank annual reports. It replaces the near-black hospitality tone with something the regulator and the chief risk officer both already trust.

02 · WHY LEDGER

Green of the books.

Accounting ledgers, bar-association seals, audit tick-marks: a deep green carries four centuries of "this has been checked." On the product side it can still cede to the existing indigo accent; brand-side, it owns the eye.

03 · WHY PAPER

File, not feed.

Warm cream keeps the editorial serif voice but grounds it in ledger paper rather than marble foyer. The faint ruled lines in the hero are a nod to a bound audit record — not decoration, documentation.
